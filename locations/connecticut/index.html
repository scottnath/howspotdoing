How's Pot Doing in Connecticut?

Cannabis is 100% legal in Connecticut

Last reviewed: January 3, 2026

Is it legal for adults to possess cannabis in the state of Connecticut?

YES

"As of July 1, 2021, possession and use of up to 1.5 oz of cannabis is legal. Connecticut residents may also store up to 5 oz in a locked container at home or transport it in a locked glovebox or trunk." - https://portal.ct.gov/cannabis

Is it legal for adults to buy cannabis in a store in the state of Connecticut?

YES

"Adult-use sales have started in connecticut at licensed retailers. Consumer protection reminds adults 21+ who purchase cannabis to use products responsibly." - https://portal.ct.gov/cannabis

Is it legal for adults to grow cannabis in the state of Connecticut?

YES

"Medical marijuana patients 18 years and older and adults 21 years of age and older are able to grow up to 3 mature and 3 immature plants in their home, with a cap of 12 total plants per household." - https://portal.ct.gov/cannabis/Knowledge-Base/Articles/Growing-cannabis-at-home

Is it legal for medical marijuana patients to possess cannabis in the state of Connecticut?

YES

"Registered medical marijuana patients may have up to 5 ounces of medical marijuana per month." - https://portal.ct.gov/DCP/Medical-Marijuana-Program/Medical-Marijuana-Program

Is it legal for medical marijuana patients to buy cannabis in a store in the state of Connecticut?

YES

"Qualifying patients and caregivers who are registered with the Department of Consumer Protection may purchase medical marijuana from a licensed dispensary facility or hybrid retailer in Connecticut." - https://portal.ct.gov/DCP/Medical-Marijuana-Program/Medical-Marijuana-Program

Is it legal for medical marijuana patients to grow cannabis in the state of Connecticut?

YES

"Any qualifying patient who is eighteen years of age or older may cultivate up to three mature cannabis plants and three immature cannabis plants in the patient's primary residence at any given time, provided such plants are secure from access by any individual other than the patient or patient's caregiver and no more than twelve cannabis plants may be grown per household." - https://www.cga.ct.gov/2023/pub/chap_420f.htm
